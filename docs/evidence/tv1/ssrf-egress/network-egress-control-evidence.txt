--- SSRF Network Egress Control Evidence ---
Docker-level network egress control is implemented in infra/docker-compose.yml.

Network-layer control:
- user-service, order-service, billing-service, and admin-service attach only to Docker networks with internal: true.
- Kong remains reachable from the host on infra_default and joins per-service internal upstream networks for backend routing.
- billing-service and order-service do not share a direct plaintext Docker network.
- billing-service reaches order-mtls-proxy over billing-order-mtls-internal.
- order-mtls-proxy reaches order-service over kong-order-internal and requires a client certificate signed by the lab CA.
- backend services do not attach to infra_default or other non-internal management networks, so they do not inherit unrestricted Internet egress.

Runtime proof:
- Script: tests/security/network-egress-control-tests.sh
- Evidence output: docs/evidence/tv1/ssrf-egress/network-egress-control-runtime-after-fix.txt
- Assertions:
  1. admin-service cannot directly reach http://169.254.169.254/latest/meta-data/
  2. admin-service cannot directly reach https://example.com
  3. billing-service can reach order-service over the approved internal S2S network
  4. backend service Docker networks are internal:true at runtime

Application-layer SSRF URL validation is separate:
- Evidence: docs/evidence/tv1/ssrf-egress/ssrf-fixed-block-metadata-ip.txt
- Control: Admin fixed endpoint rejects metadata/private/internal URLs before fetch.
